    Eaton Power Management

    HCI capstone project to envision the future of interacting with switchgear

    Scope: HCI Capstone Project

    Client: Eaton

    Target Users: Service Engineers working with Switchgear

    Team: Pierre Amelot, Gyuri Han, Arnita Saini, Neeraj Talukdar, Caroline Win

    My Role: Project Manager & Design Consultant

    I am the primary contact with the client and create agendas for team meetings held 2-3 times per week. I also coordinate logistics for events such as our client kickoff meeting.

    Additionally, I support the team in conducting research and design workshops.

    Timeline: Jan - Aug 2017

    Hunt Statement

    Create the future of human-switchgear interaction to enhance safety, reliability, efficiency, and the human experience of power management systems using emerging technologies.

    What are switchgear?

    Switchgear are apparatuses used to protect and control electrical equipment. They can contain many components including circuit breakers, fuses, and protective relays. Within a certain distance from switchgear, there can be risk of an arc flash, a type of electrical explosion.

    Project Plan for Spring

    Currently, our team is working on conducting secondary research, including literature review and internet research on switchgear and relevant stakeholders. We are also conducting a competitive analyses on similar products and exploring analagous domains to see how other industries use technology to improve the experience of interacting with complex and dangerous equipment.
