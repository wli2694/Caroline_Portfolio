    AVMOSYS

    Mobile app for business aviation management

    Due to confidentiality reasons, screenshots of the application are not included on this page. Can be made available upon request.

    Please contact me if interested.

    Client: Argus International, Inc.

    Target Users: Business Aviation Professionals

    Team: 3 developers, 1 customer experience manager, 1 designer (me)

    My Role: UI / UX Designer

    Dates: Nov 2015 - Nov 2016

    Goals

    My team's goal was to design and develop a mobile version of AVMOSYS, Argus's current web application for aviation management. This software allows business aviation professionals such as sales representatives, scheduler, and operations managers to calculate trip quotes and schedule and coordinate highly complex and customized trips for clients.

    My goal was to guide the design process to optimize user experience and design the user interface for the app.

    Research

    My first step was to get to know my team and understand the structure of the company. I did this by meeting with different coworkers and asking questions. I also attended a few orientation sessions on the history of AVMOSYS and business aviation.

    Stakeholder Interviews

    I met with the leaders of my team and the company CIO and asked many questions about the goals of the project, their ideas and visions for the project, and current and expected challenges. I also asked about their expections of me.

    Learning the Current Software

    To better understand the application I would be helping to design, I spent a few days going through every single tab, page, field, and button in the curent web application and evaluating the design and usability. I printed screenshots and wrote notes on the pages.

    User Research

    I met with the Customer Experience Manager on my team to gain insights about the customer complaints and needs. I was able to determine the primary user group through speaking with her - Aviation Schedulers.

    The following month, I visited one of Argus's clients and interviewed sales representatives, schedulers, and operations managers. I observed them carrying out their normal work processes.

    Design

    I then began to sketch ideas for the mobile interface and then created wireframes for particular functions. I shared my designs and received feedback from the team at our daily meetings. My main goal at this point was to create as many ideas as possible to encourage discussion and creativity among team members.

    Interactive Mockups

    Using Sketch, I created higher-fidelity mockups and added interactivity with InvisionApp to allow team members to test the usability of our team's ideas and to give an idea of the direction we plan to take to other employees at the company.

    In the mean time, the developers began to work on building the parts of the application we decided on as a team.

    Reflection

    Overall this has been an extremely valuable and exciting experience for me. The members of my team were all such amazing and fun people who respected each others' opinions and loved to communicate openly and bounce ideas off of each other. I think I have been very lucky to work in this environment.

    A few of the challenges included:

    • Business aviation - a highly complex field with many meticulous tasks and expectaitons
    • Differences in opinion within argus about design and approach for AVMOSYS
    • Being an entry-level designer but also the only designer in the company
    • Explaining design topics and topics to co-workers unfamiliar with design
